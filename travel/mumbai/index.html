Mumbai is a colorful and mix of many religions city. With full of unique cultures and a great history, this is one of the best places that should be in your travel journey.
Let's Explore!

Mumbai is a city which is famous for their musical culture. People here love to sing Marathi Koli songs and perform the classical Bharatanatyam dance. Everyday in Mumbai is like a musical show.

All the Mumbaikars are always united in the grand celebrations such as The Ganesha festival – the Elephant God day, the Holi festival – where people throw colors to each other in order to share their love.

Mumbai is famous for their different types of food, from the traditional one includes Potato bhaji, Batatya rassa, to the unique types of sweets that are specially prepared in auspicious occasion, like Modak, Basundi.

The Bollywood – the name combined by Bombay (formal name of Mumbai) and Hollywood, was born in Mumbai. Here, the Hindu cinema and Bollywood industry are considered to be an entertainment lifeline of India.

THE GATEWAY OF INDIA

Located in south of Mumbai, it is the most unique India monument decorated in Muslim architecture with traditional Hindu textiles. It was constructed in 1924 in order to commemorate the visit of King George C and Queen Mary from England. Open 24/7, tourists can come anytime to contemplate this special landmark.

SANJAY GANDHI NATIONAL PARK

Spreading over 100 km2, this is the home of 800 flowering plants, 284 bird varieties, 40 species of mammal and several other types of plants and wildlife, including many endangered species such as the Bengal tigers. Besides, the Kanheri caves is an excellent choice for rock climbers.

MUMBAI FILM CITY

Built by the Maharashtra state government in 1978, this place has provided facilities for the Bollywood film industry with 20 indoor fully-equipped studios and outdoor settings for filming. Tourists can visit through local tours and if you’re lucky, you can see some live Bollywood shooting.

CHOWPATTY BEACH

Walking on this glorious beach can give you a chance to see some monkey dance, snake-charmers and fortune tellers. Specially, their wonderful street vendor food is very unique, diversified and yet, yummy tummy. Best time to visit is between October and March, when you can attend the amazing Ganesh Chaturthi festival.
